Double-Slit Geometry // Interactive Companion

DEMOS 25–32

Eight interactive diagrams, one per load-bearing piece of “Double-Slit Geometry: Definitions, Axioms, and Theorems” and its companion note “The Seam.” Every demo runs the same calibrated rig (Zv = 150, Zh = 250), is a single dependency-free HTML file, works with touch, and ships with a headless verification script (node) that checks its mathematics to machine precision — the same discipline as geometry_checks.py. Read a section, then drag the corresponding picture.

Bowing Theorem

Theorem 3 · all three cases
Drag a line by its endpoints; its chain updates live with exact Möbius coefficients, axis-parallel asymptotes color-coded to the slit-plane crossing that causes each. Straighten (c = Dz·D, so case 2 is “parallel to the screen”) or steer into a slit for the cross.
Finding: case 3 is the limit of case 1 — the hyperbola collapses onto its own asymptotes.

Chain Criterion

Theorem 6b · D8 · Theorem 4
Four draggable rays; λ and µ live with a skew-defect meter. The chain of r₁r₂r₃ predicts r₄; in 3D the full regulus of transversals sketches the quadric. Snap co-chained and every member touches all four — the bright transversal rides the surface.
Finding: the 3D co-chained test must use the max gap over the regulus — a generic line stabs the quadric twice, so two members always touch.
verified by check_chaincriterion.js · λ=µ forward to 2e−13

§7–8  The Hierarchy and its Degenerations

31

Pinhole Collapse

Theorem 8 · Degeneration Table, row 1 → 2
Walk the slit separation h to zero: the pinhole O fades in, four rays re-concentrate, and the test chain straightens against its chord (c = Dz·h identically). A log-log panel plots bow, |λ−µ|, and the pairwise ray gap all dying at slope 1.
Finding: µ is exactly h-independent — only λ migrates; and fusion requires the rays coplanar with O (generic tuples plateau, correctly).

The Seam  Shared Feet and Exiled Meetings

32

Incidence Inversion

Seam §4–5 · the algebra of ℝ⊕ℝ
Two rays: joinable pairs carry a sweeping one-parameter family of chains, each the image of a real line m shown touching both rays in space. Below, the difference plane ℝ⊕ℝ with the zero-divisor axes as the light cone: drag δ onto an axis and the rays meet, the chains die, N(δ) hits zero.
Findings: the 3D gap has the closed form D|Δs·Δt|/√(D²Δs²+D²Δt²+(s₁t₂−s₂t₁)²) — the split-norm is the miss; and the forced fit through 1-parallel rays poles exactly at the shared foot.
verified by check_incidence.js · gap identity to 8.5e−14

—  Not built, by design

The standalone Regulus Ambiguity (Theorem 4) is absorbed into demo 29, which draws the full ruling; the bi-cross-ratio taxonomy appears as live tags in the same demo; the Klein hierarchy (§7) remains a table, as it should. Findings that belong back in the papers are collected in the addenda.

All demos share the calibrated rig of the Tetra Engine (screen z=0, ℓ₁ vertical at Zv=150, ℓ₂ horizontal at Zh=250) and a common plain-canvas renderer — no libraries, no network. Companion to the Tetra Engine project · flyingsticks.github.io. Interaction conventions throughout: drag handles to move geometry, drag empty space to orbit, wheel or pinch to zoom.
