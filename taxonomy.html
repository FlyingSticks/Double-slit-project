Double-Slit Geometry · Organizing the Readings

What Is a Geometry?

Two things are being conflated when we say "three readings," and separating them makes the whole picture click. There are two methods for defining a geometry — Hilbert's and Klein's — describing one and the same plane. And inside Klein's method there is a ladder: nominate more structure, the group shrinks, the invariants sharpen. "Calibrated" is the bottom rung of that ladder, not a third method.

The two methods are not rivals — they are two descriptions of the same double-slit plane, and the companion proves it both ways. Theorem 2: the synthetic axioms (a Minkowski plane closing Miquel) force the classical model — chain geometry over ℝ⊕ℝ. Theorem 5.4: conversely, every automorphism of that structure is induced by an element of G. Synthetic ⇄ transformational, certified in both directions. Everything below refines the Klein side.

Axis 2 — The Erlangen ladder (inside the Klein method)

Tap a rung. Down the ladder: nominate more structure → the group shrinks → invariants get finer. Between Level 2 and Level 3, descriptions become measurements.

The classical Erlangen tower is projective ⊃ affine ⊃ Euclidean; each step nominates more (the plane at infinity, then the metric) and shrinks the group. The double-slit ladder is that same tower with one skew-line pair held sacred — which is why fixing the slit pair drops the ambient PGL(4) (dim 15) to G (dim 7) before the ladder even begins.
