The Chain

A straight line, seen through two slits, does not stay straight — it bows into a chain: the graph of a Möbius map between the slits, and the split-signature sibling of the circle. Two views of one object.

D4In space — the definition

ℓ₁, ℓ₂ are two skew slits — the seam. A chain is every ray meeting one further line m (gold) skew to both. The rays thread the slits, bow, and are pairwise skew — no two meet (C2). Drag to rotate.

Thm 3On the screen — the bowing

Each ray is a point (s, t); the chain is the graph t = φ(s) — a hyperbola whose asymptotes cross at the center ray q. Drag the three rays: three fix one chain (M1). Slide them collinear so c → 0 and the chain straightens to a line — Euclid, recovered.

Theorem — verified

The image of a line is a chain: the graph of a Möbius map (Thm 3). Three pairwise-non-parallel rays fix it (M1). Four rays share a chain iff λ = µ (Thm 6b). Checked in geometry_checks.py.

Observation

c → 0 straightens the chain to a line — Euclid recovered as the limiting aperture. Merge two feet and the pole runs to the shared foot; the join degenerates to the cross.

Rhyme — not identity

The chain plays the role "circle" plays in the inversive plane, over the double numbers ℝ ⊕ ℝ instead of ℂ (Benz's Minkowski plane). A structural rhyme, held apart from the theorems above.
